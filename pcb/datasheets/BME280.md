# BME280 / BMP280 (U1) — Environmental Sensor

> Datasheet extracted: Bosch BME280 Data sheet, Document rev 1.23, BST-BME280-DS001-23 (Jan 2022).
> Source PDF: `bme280.pdf`.

> **IMPORTANT — part-number mismatch to confirm:** This board's schematic symbol/value is **BMP280**
> (pressure + temperature ONLY, **no humidity**). The datasheet extracted here is the **BME280**
> (adds a humidity sensor). The two parts share the **same LGA-8 package, identical pinout, and the
> same I²C 7-bit addresses (0x76/0x77)**, and the BME280 is "register and performance compatible to
> BMP280" (p.2). For PCB/schematic-review purposes (pinout, supply, decoupling, interface, address
> straps) they are interchangeable. The only board-relevant differences are firmware-side: chip ID
> register `0xD0` reads **0x58 (BMP280 mass production)** vs **0x60 (BME280)**, and the BME280 has
> humidity registers/features the BMP280 lacks (p.26). **Confirm whether BMP280 or BME280 is actually
> being placed before ordering/firmware.**

## Overview
The BME280/BMP280 is a Bosch digital environmental sensor in a 2.5 mm × 2.5 mm × 0.93 mm metal-lid
**LGA-8** package (p.2). The BME280 measures humidity, barometric pressure and temperature; the
BMP280 (the board's marked part) measures pressure and temperature only. Both support I²C
(up to 3.4 MHz) and SPI (3- and 4-wire, up to 10 MHz) (p.2). On this smartwatch it is U1, the
environmental sensor, intended to sit on the I²C bus alongside the other sensors. Supply VDD
1.71–3.6 V, interface VDDIO 1.2–3.6 V (p.2). Listed target devices explicitly include "Watches" (p.1).

## Pinout
LGA-8. Pin numbering is clockwise in top view, counter-clockwise in bottom view (p.38). Table 35 (p.38):

| Pin # | Name | Type | Function | Connect-to / notes |
|-------|------|------|----------|--------------------|
| 1 | GND | ground | Ground | GND (p.38) |
| 2 | CSB | digital-IO (In) | Chip select | **I²C: tie to VDDIO** (selects I²C, active-low chip-select for SPI). For I²C a *direct* connection to VDDIO is required (p.38, p.39) |
| 3 | SDI | digital-IO (In/Out) | Serial data in; **SDA in I²C**; data I/O in 3-wire SPI | I²C: SDA, open-drain, needs external pull-up to VDDIO (p.32, p.38) |
| 4 | SCK | digital-IO (In) | Serial clock; **SCL in I²C** | I²C: SCL, needs external pull-up to VDDIO (p.32, p.38) |
| 5 | SDO | digital-IO (In/Out) | Serial data out; **I²C address LSB select** | I²C: GND → addr 0x76, VDDIO → addr 0x77. **Must NOT float** in I²C (addr undefined). SPI 4W: SDO; SPI 3W: DNC (p.31, p.32, p.38) |
| 6 | VDDIO | power | Digital / interface supply | VDDIO rail + 100 nF decoupling (C2) (p.38, p.39) |
| 7 | GND | ground | Ground | GND (p.38) |
| 8 | VDD | power | Analog supply | VDD rail + 100 nF decoupling (C1) (p.38, p.39) |

Note: there is also a **vent hole** in the package lid (shown on pin-out, p.38) — keep it unobstructed
for pressure/humidity sensing; mechanical/assembly consideration, not an electrical pin.

## Power & supply
- **VDD (analog/main) operating range:** 1.71 V (min) / 1.8 V (typ) / 3.6 V (max), VDD ripple max
  50 mVpp (Table 1, p.8).
- **VDDIO (interface) operating range:** 1.2 V (min) / 1.8 V (typ) / 3.6 V (max) (Table 1, p.8).
- **Absolute maximum ratings (Table 5, p.13):**
  - Voltage at any supply pin (VDD, VDDIO): **−0.3 V to +4.25 V**
  - Voltage at any interface pin: **−0.3 V to VDDIO + 0.3 V**
  - Storage temperature: −45 to +85 °C (≤65% RH)
  - Pressure: 0 to 20 000 hPa
  - ESD HBM ±2 kV, CDM ±500 V, Machine model ±200 V
- **Decoupling:** recommended **100 nF** on VDD (C1) and **100 nF** on VDDIO (C2), one cap per supply
  pin (p.39, p.40, p.41).
- **Start-up / sequencing:** start-up time tstartup ≤ 2 ms, defined as time to first communication after
  *both* VDD > 1.58 V and VDDIO > 0.65 V (Table 1, p.8). Device startup is deferred until both VDD and
  VDDIO are established, so there is no risk of incorrect protocol (I²C/SPI) detection from supply
  sequencing (p.32). No specific VDD-vs-VDDIO ordering is mandated; "not stated in datasheet" beyond this.

## Configuration / strap & mode pins
Two hardware-strap pins: **CSB** selects the bus, **SDO** selects the I²C address.

**Interface selection via CSB (p.32):**

| CSB strap | Interface selected |
|-----------|--------------------|
| Connected to VDDIO | I²C active |
| Pulled down (low) | SPI active |

> Latch behavior: once CSB has been pulled low even once (with or without clock), I²C is **disabled
> until the next power-on-reset** to avoid mis-decoding SPI as I²C (p.32). For I²C, CSB must already be
> at VDDIO level during power-on-reset — if CSB is driven by a GPIO that is not high at POR, the part
> locks into SPI mode and will not answer I²C (p.32). Hence a **direct CSB→VDDIO connection is
> required for I²C** (p.39).

**I²C address selection via SDO (p.31, p.32):** 7-bit address = `111011x`, 6 MSB fixed, LSB = SDO.

| SDO strap | 7-bit I²C address |
|-----------|-------------------|
| GND ('0') | `1110110` = **0x76** (default) |
| VDDIO ('1') | `1110111` = **0x77** (same as BMP280's address) |

> SDO **cannot be left floating** in I²C mode — the address would be undefined (p.32). SDO can be
> changed during operation (it doubles as the address LSB) (p.31).

**SPI mode (firmware, register `0xF5` config bit `spi3w_en`):** 3-wire SPI is selected by writing '1'
to `spi3w_en`; 4-wire is the default (p.34). Not a hardware strap.

## Interface
- **I²C** (p.31, p.32): slave only; supports Standard, Fast, and High-Speed modes (up to 3.4 MHz).
  7-bit address 0x76 (SDO=GND) or 0x77 (SDO=VDDIO). SDA (SDI) and SCL (SCK) are **not pure open-drain**
  but contain ESD diodes to VDDIO/GND; no clock stretching (SCL is high-Z input). SDA must be pulled up
  to VDDIO externally. Write = address `111011X0`; read = `111011X1` with register auto-increment.
- **SPI** (p.34): slave only, 4-wire or 3-wire. Supports **mode '00' (CPOL=CPHA=0)** and
  **mode '11' (CPOL=CPHA=1)**; auto-selected by SCK level at the CSB falling edge. **Max SCK = 10 MHz**
  (F_spi, Table 34, p.37). Data on SDI latched on SCK rising edge; SDO changes on SCK falling edge. CSB
  has an integrated pull-up. 3-wire mode: SDI is the bidirectional data pad, SDO is hi-Z (set via
  `spi3w_en`). SPI register address uses 7 bits; MSB replaced by R/W (0=write, 1=read) (p.34).
- **Protocol auto-detect:** selection is purely by CSB state at/after POR (see strap table above).

## Application circuit / external components
From the I²C connection diagram (Figure 17, p.39) and SPI diagrams (p.40, p.41):

| Component | Value | Where | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|-------|
| C1 (VDD decoupling) | **100 nF** | VDD (pin 8) to GND | Recommended (p.39–41) |
| C2 (VDDIO decoupling) | **100 nF** | VDDIO (pin 6) to GND | Recommended (p.39–41) |
| R1, R2 (I²C pull-ups) | **4.7 kΩ typ** | SDA (pin 3) and SCL (pin 4) to VDDIO | "should be based on interface timing and bus load; a normal value is 4.7 kΩ" (p.39). Max bus load cap Cb = 400 pF on SDI+SCK (Table 32, p.35) |

No inductors, diodes, or sense resistors are required (passive sensor; "not applicable").

## Key electrical specs
Interface logic levels are ratiometric to VDDIO (Table 32, p.35):
- **VIL (Vil_si):** max **20 %VDDIO** (input low)
- **VIH (Vih_si):** min **80 %VDDIO** (input high)
- **VOL (I²C, SDI):** ≤20 %VDDIO @ Iol=3 mA, VDDIO=1.62 V (≤23 %VDDIO @ VDDIO=1.2 V)
- **VOL (SPI, SDO):** ≤20 %VDDIO @ Iol=1 mA, VDDIO=1.62 V
- **VOH (SDO/SDI):** ≥80 %VDDIO @ Ioh=1 mA, VDDIO=1.62 V (≥60 %VDDIO @ VDDIO=1.2 V)
- **CSB internal pull-up (to VDDIO):** 70 / 120 / 190 kΩ (Rpull) — relevant only in SPI use
- **Max I²C bus capacitance (Cb):** 400 pF on SDI and SCK

Currents (Table 1 p.8; Tables 2–3 p.9–10):
- **Sleep current (IDDSL):** 0.1 µA typ / 0.3 µA max
- **Standby current (IDDSB, normal-mode inactive):** 0.2 µA typ / 0.5 µA max
- **1 Hz forced, humidity+temp:** 1.8 µA typ / 2.8 µA max (BME280 only)
- **1 Hz forced, pressure+temp (lowest power):** 2.8 µA typ / 4.2 µA max
- Peak measurement currents: IDDH 340 µA (humidity), IDDP 714 µA (pressure), IDDT 350 µA (temp) (p.8)
- **tstartup:** ≤ 2 ms to first communication (p.8)

Chip ID (`0xD0`): **0x60 = BME280**; **0x58 = BMP280 mass production** (0x56/0x57 = BMP280 samples) (p.26).

## Schematic-review checklist
- [ ] **Confirm BMP280 vs BME280 intent.** Board symbol/value is BMP280 (no humidity). If humidity is
      actually needed, the part must be BME280. Pinout/footprint/address are identical, so the board
      works either way — but firmware chip-ID check (0x58 vs 0x60) and humidity feature differ (p.26).
- [ ] **CSB (pin 2) must be tied directly to VDDIO** for I²C. Do NOT leave floating and do NOT route to a
      GPIO unless that GPIO is guaranteed high at POR — otherwise the part locks into SPI mode and never
      answers I²C (p.32, p.39).
- [ ] **SDO (pin 5) must be strapped GND or VDDIO** — never floating in I²C (address undefined) (p.32).
      Verify the chosen address (0x76 SDO=GND / 0x77 SDO=VDDIO) does not collide with another I²C device.
      Note **0x77 is also BMP280's default** — watch for conflicts with any other Bosch sensor on the bus.
- [ ] **I²C pull-ups on SDA(pin3) and SCL(pin4) to VDDIO** present (≈4.7 kΩ); SDA/SCL are not pure
      open-drain but still need pull-ups (p.32, p.39). Keep total bus cap ≤400 pF (p.35).
- [ ] **Decoupling:** 100 nF on VDD (pin 8) and 100 nF on VDDIO (pin 6), each close to the pin (p.39–41).
- [ ] **Both GND pins (1 and 7) connected** to ground plane (p.38).
- [ ] **Pull-ups go to VDDIO, not VDD.** Confirm VDDIO matches the host MCU/bus logic level (1.2–3.6 V)
      and that VDDIO and VDD rails are within range (VDD 1.71–3.6 V, VDDIO 1.2–3.6 V) (p.8).
- [ ] **Absolute max:** no supply pin above 4.25 V, no interface pin above VDDIO+0.3 V (p.13).
- [ ] **Vent hole** in package lid must be left open (no conformal coating / no component over it) for
      pressure/humidity sensing (p.38).
- [ ] If SPI were used instead: it's mode 0 or mode 3, ≤10 MHz; CSB has an internal pull-up; SDO is hi-Z
      in 3-wire mode (p.34, p.37). (Board is presumably I²C — verify.)
