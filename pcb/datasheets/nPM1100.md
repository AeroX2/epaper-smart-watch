# nPM1100 (IC1) — PMIC: USB Li-ion charger + buck regulator

> Source: nPM1100_PS_v1_3-3114436.pdf (Nordic Semiconductor, v1.3). Page numbers cited inline are the
> **printed** datasheet page numbers (PDF footer "4445_367 v1.3 NN"). This board uses the **QFN 4.0x4.0 mm
> 24-pin** variant (`nPM1100-QDAx`), so the QFN24 pin table (Table 24, p.48) is authoritative.

## Overview
The nPM1100 is an integrated Power Management IC (PMIC): a linear-mode Li-ion/Li-poly USB battery charger
(up to 400 mA), a 5 V input LDO/system regulator (SYSREG) with USB port detection and VBUS current limiting,
and a high-efficiency step-down buck regulator (VOUTB, pin-selectable 1.8/2.1/2.7/3.0 V, up to 150 mA).
It requires **no software/control interface** — all configuration is by hardware straps and external resistors
(p.iii). Package on this board: QFN 4.0x4.0 mm, 24 leads + exposed pad, 0.5 mm pitch (Table 32, p.59). On this
e-paper smartwatch it is IC1, charging the Li-po battery from USB VBUS and supplying the rail to the STM32WB5MMG /
peripherals via VOUTB (and/or VSYS). Standard-VTERM product (`-QDAA`) gives 4.1/4.2 V termination; high-VTERM
(`-QDAB`) gives 4.25/4.35 V (Table 33, p.59) — **verify which option is ordered against the cell's max charge
voltage.**

- The functional ground pins are **AVSS** (analog, exposed pad / WLCSP balls C4,C5) and **PVSS** (DC/DC, pin 23).
  Abs-max specifies AVSS/PVSS = 0 V (Table 4, p.13).
- "VOUTBSET" naming: Table 18 (p.31) uses **VOUTBSET1** (MSB) and **VOUTBSET0** (LSB). The QFN24 pin table

## Power & supply
- **VBUS (pin 17)** recommended operating: 4.1 V (min) / 5 V (nom) / 6.7 V (max) (Table 7, p.15). Absolute max:
  **-0.3 V to +20 V** (Table 4, p.13); transient/OVP tolerant to 20 V (p.17). OVP threshold VBUSOVP ≈ 6.9 V,
  UVLO VBUSMIN ≈ 3.9 V (Table 11, p.19).
- **VBAT (pin 15)** recommended operating: 2.30 V to 4.35 V (Table 7, p.15). Absolute max: **-0.3 V to +5.5 V**
  (Table 4, p.13).
- **VSYS, DEC, SW** absolute max: **-0.3 V to +5.5 V** (Table 4, p.13). VSYS system output 3.0–5.5 V (p.iii).
- **VANAI/O** (D-, D+, NTC, ICHG, VOUTB) and **VDIGI/O** (VOUTBSET0, VOUTBSET1, VTERMSET, SHPHLD, SHPACT, ISET,
  ERR, CHG, MODE) absolute max: **-0.3 V to VINT + 0.3 V** (Table 4, p.13).
- **Decoupling / required external caps (from reference BOM, Tables 28-30, p.51-53):**
  - VBUS (pin 17): **2.2 µF**, X5R, 25 V (C1)
  - VSYS (pin 16): **10 µF**, X5R, 6.3 V (Config 3, C2/C3/C4)
  - DEC (pin 21): **1.0 µF** (Config 1 names this C3, 10 V; Config 3 C5)
  - VOUTB (pin 1): **10 µF** (Config 3); output-cap effective range 6–20 µF (Table 21, p.32)
  - VBAT (pin 15): **1.0 µF**, X5R, 10 V (C3 in Config 1)
  - SW→VOUTB inductor: **2.2 µH**, ≤20%, DCR ≤400 mΩ, Isat/Imax ≥350 mA (Table 20, p.32)
  - Note: a minimum of five passive components are required for operation (p.iii).
- **Sequencing:** No external supply allowed on VSYS or DEC — they are internally driven (p.17). POR occurs when
  VBUS > VBUSPOR (3.9 V) **or** VBAT > VBATPOR (2.7 V); BOR when VBUS < VBUSBOR (3.8 V) **and** VBAT < VBATBOR
  (2.5 V) (p.10, Tables 11/16). VSYS auto-enables after POR; BUCK enables once VINT > VINTBUCKMIN (~2.8 V, Table 22).

## Configuration / strap & mode pins

### BUCK output-voltage select — Table 18 (p.31)
VOUTBSET pins must be hardwired to DEC, VSYS, or AVSS; **do not toggle during operation** (p.31).
LOW = tie to AVSS; HIGH = tie to VSYS or DEC.

| VOUTBSET1 (pin 2, MSB) | VOUTBSET0 (pin 3, LSB) | VOUTB |
|------------------------|------------------------|-------|
| LOW  | LOW  | 1.8 V |
| LOW  | HIGH | 2.1 V |
| HIGH | LOW  | 2.7 V |
| HIGH | HIGH | 3.0 V |

(QFN pin 3 is labelled "VOUTBSET2" in Table 24 but is functionally VOUTBSET0/LSB — see Pinout note.)

### BUCK mode select (MODE, pin 7) — Table 19 (p.32)
| MODE | BUCK operation |
|------|----------------|
| LOW  | Automatic (Hysteretic ↔ PWM by load) |
| HIGH | Forced PWM (constant FBUCK ≈ 3.6 MHz, low ripple, RF-friendly) |

MODE may be changed at any time (dynamic) (p.32).

### Termination-voltage select (VTERMSET, pin 9) — Table 12 (p.22)
**Toggle only when device is in Power OFF** (Table 24). Depends on ordered product option (Table 33).

| Product option (`<VV>`) | VTERMSET | VTERM |
|-------------------------|----------|-------|
| Standard VTERM (`-QDAA`) | LOW  | 4.1 V |
| Standard VTERM (`-QDAA`) | HIGH | 4.2 V |
| High VTERM (`-QDAB`)     | LOW  | 4.25 V |
| High VTERM (`-QDAB`)     | HIGH | 4.35 V |

Function-variant codes (Table 33, p.59): `AA` = Standard VTERM, `AB` = High VTERM.

### Charge-current select (ICHG resistor, pin 12) — Table 13 (p.23)
R_ICHG connected between ICHG and AVSS. Valid 0 Ω–30 kΩ; range 20–400 mA.

| R_ICHG | ICHGLIM (nominal) |
|--------|-------------------|
| 0 Ω (short to AVSS) | 400 mA |
| 1.5 kΩ | 200 mA |
| 4.7 kΩ | 100 mA |
| 11 kΩ  | 50 mA |
| 30 kΩ  | 20 mA |

**ICHGLIM must be ≤ the battery's safe charge-current limit** (p.23).

### VBUS current-limit select (ISET / D+ / D-) — Table 10 (p.18)
| Method | Pin config | VBUS current limit |
|--------|-----------|--------------------|
| Fixed 100 mA | ISET = AVSS, D- = AVSS, D+ = NC | 100 mA |
| Fixed 500 mA | ISET = VSYS, D- = AVSS, D+ = NC | 500 mA |
| USB port detection | ISET = AVSS; D+ & D- to host | 100 mA (SDP) / 500 mA (DCP/CDP) |
| Detection + negotiation | ISET = MCU GPIO; D+ & D- to host | SDP: 100 mA (ISET LOW) or 500 mA (ISET HIGH); DCP/CDP: 500 mA |

When ISET is GPIO-driven: it **must be LOW on reset and when USB disconnected**; only set HIGH after SDP
negotiation completes (p.18).

### Ship mode (SHPACT pin 6 / SHPHLD pin 11) — §3.5 (p.10)
- Enter: SHPACT high ≥ tactiveToShip (200 ms) with VBUS disconnected and SHPHLD high. SHPACT has internal pull-down.
- Exit: connect VBUS, or pull SHPHLD low ≥ tshipToActive (200 ms). In ship mode VBAT holds SHPHLD high via weak pull-up.
- **If Ship mode not required, tie SHPACT and SHPHLD to AVSS** (p.10).

### NTC config (pin 14) — §6.2.5 / §7.6 (p.24, p.43)
- With thermal protection: 10 kΩ NTC thermistor (1%, B25/50 = 3380 K) between NTC and AVSS, thermally coupled to cell (Table 14).
- **If thermal protection NOT used: connect a 10 kΩ (≤20%) resistor between NTC and AVSS** (p.24). (Do not float.)

## Interface
**No digital control bus.** The nPM1100 "functions without a control interface" — there is no I2C or SPI; all
configuration is by hardware straps and resistors, and status is reported only via the open-drain CHG/ERR LED
pins (p.iii, §3.1.1 Table 2). This is N/A for I2C/SPI address review.

Power-up protocol detection: SYSREG performs automatic **USB BC1.2 port detection** (SDP/DCP/CDP) starting on
VBUS connect, completing after TCONN0 (~700 ms with ISET=LOW). The host MCU must wait for detection to finish
before enabling its own USB port and before raising ISET for 500 mA negotiation (p.18, §7.3 p.43).

## Application circuit / external components
From the QFN reference schematics + BOMs (Figs 40/42/44, Tables 28/29/30, p.51-53). Three reference
configurations are provided; the smartwatch most closely matches **Configuration 3 (normal / USB port
detection, BUCK + Ship + NTC configured)**.

Configuration 3 BOM (Table 30, p.53):
- C1 (VBUS): **2.2 µF**, X5R, 25 V, ±20%, 0603
- C2, C3, C4 (VSYS, DEC, VOUTB): **10 µF**, X5R, 6.3 V, ±20%, 0603
- C5 (VBAT): **1.0 µF**, X5R, 10 V, ±20%, 0201
- L1 (SW→VOUTB): **2.2 µH** inductor, ±20%, 0806
- R_ICHG (ICHG→GND): **1.5 kΩ** 1% (→ 200 mA charge) — *adjust per battery; Table 13*
- R3: 1 kΩ (0201) is only for the optional VBUS-present indicator LED LD3. The CHG/ERR LEDs LD1/LD2 connect directly to VSYS because those pins regulate their sink current to 5 mA.
- J1: battery pack with NTC (TP_3x1mm_TH)
- ISET = VSYS (optional), D_N/D_P to USB host, MODE/SHPACT/SHPHLD as configured

Configuration 1/2 (minimal, BUCK unused, fixed VBUS limit) instead use: C1 2.2 µF (VBUS), C2 22 µF (VSYS),
C3 1.0 µF (DEC/VBAT), R1 10 kΩ, R_ICHG 4.7 kΩ (Config1, 100 mA) or 0 Ω (Config2, 400 mA), with VTERMSET=LOW,
D+ = NC. (Tables 28/29, p.51-52.) Use these only if the BUCK is not used — **this board uses the BUCK, so follow
Configuration 3.**

Battery pack requirement: packs on VBAT must include overcharge protection, undervoltage protection, overcurrent
discharge fuse, and a thermal fuse if no NTC is present (p.11). Application must **not** be powered directly from
VBAT — use VOUTB and/or VSYS (p.42).

## Key electrical specs
- **Logic thresholds:** VIH = 1.1 V (min) to VINT; VIL = 0 to 0.4 V (max) (Table 3, p.12).
- **VBUS current limits:** IBUSLIM1 (CDP/DCP or ISET=HIGH) 450–500 mA; IBUSLIM0 (SDP, ISET=LOW @25 °C) 90–100 mA (Table 11, p.19).
- **Charge current:** configurable 20–400 mA via R_ICHG; accuracy ±10% (ICHG ≥50 mA) / ±15% (<50 mA) (Tables 13/16, p.23/26).
- **Termination current ITERM:** 8/10/12 % of ICHG; trickle current 10% of ICHG (Tables 16/17, p.26-27).
- **Termination voltage accuracy:** ±1% (Table 16, p.26).
- **Quiescent current:** IQBAT (battery op, no load, MODE=LOW, VBUS off) ~800 nA; IQSHIP (ship mode) ~460 nA (Table 3, p.12).
- **Discharge current limit from battery IBATLIM:** ~660 mA (Table 17, p.27).
- **BUCK:** VOUTB accuracy -2/+8%; max output ~150 mA; FBUCK ≈ 3.6 MHz; ripple ≤10 mVpp (PWM) / ≤80 mVpp (hyst);
  VINTBUCKMIN ≈ 2.8 V; VDROPOUT_BUCK ≈ 0.41 V; efficiency up to ~93.5% (Table 22, p.33).
- **VBUS thresholds:** OVP 6.9 V, UVLO 3.9 V, ULP entry 1.8 V, POR 3.9 V, BOR 3.8 V (Table 11, p.19).
- **VBAT thresholds:** VBATPOR 2.7 V, VBATBOR 2.5 V (Table 17, p.27).
- **Thermal shutdown TSD:** ~120 °C (hyst 10 °C) (Table 3, p.12). Charger thermal regulation THIGH ~100 °C → IREDUCED (Table 17, p.27).
- **CHG/ERR sink current:** 5 mA each (Table 17, p.27).
- **QFN thermal:** RθJA = 33.5 °C/W (Table 9, p.15).

## Schematic-review checklist
- **Confirm package/order code:** must be `nPM1100-QDAx` (QFN24). Verify Standard vs High VTERM (`-QDAA` vs `-QDAB`)
  matches the Li-po cell's max charge voltage — wrong variant overcharges/undercharges (Tables 12/33).
- **VOUTBSET straps:** Pin 2 (VOUTBSET1, MSB) and Pin 3 (labelled "VOUTBSET2" in Table 24 but functionally
  VOUTBSET0, LSB) must each be hardwired to AVSS (LOW) or VSYS/DEC (HIGH) — **never floating, never toggled in
  operation.** Double-check the bit ordering against Table 18 to get the intended VOUTB (1.8/2.1/2.7/3.0 V).
- **VTERMSET (pin 9):** hardwired HIGH or LOW; confirm resulting VTERM is safe for the cell. Datasheet warns to
  toggle only in Power OFF.
- **R_ICHG (pin 12 → AVSS):** present and sized for the cell (e.g. 1.5 kΩ→200 mA, 4.7 kΩ→100 mA). Must be ≤ cell's
  max charge current. 0 Ω = 400 mA. Missing/floating ICHG resistor mis-sets charge current.
- **NTC (pin 14):** either a 10 kΩ NTC to AVSS, or — if no NTC — a **10 kΩ fixed resistor NTC→AVSS**. Floating NTC
  will mis-trigger JEITA temperature protection.
- **ISET (pin 5):** strapped (AVSS/VSYS) or GPIO-driven per Table 10. If GPIO-driven, ensure firmware keeps it LOW
  on reset/USB-disconnect. For USB detection, ISET=AVSS and D+/D- routed to host.
- **D+/D- (pins 19/20):** for fixed-limit configs, D- = AVSS and **D+ = NC**; for detection, both to USB host.
  Don't leave D- floating in fixed mode.
- **SHPACT (pin 6) / SHPHLD (pin 11):** if Ship mode is unused, **tie both to AVSS.** SHPACT has internal pull-down;
  SHPHLD needs a defined level. Don't leave floating.
- **Decoupling present and correct:** VBUS 2.2 µF, VSYS 10 µF, DEC 1.0 µF, VOUTB 10 µF (6–20 µF effective), VBAT
  1.0 µF, all close to pins. Inductor 2.2 µH on SW (Isat ≥350 mA).
- **Do NOT externally drive VSYS (pin 16) or DEC (pin 21)** — they are internal outputs.
- **Do NOT power the application from VBAT** — feed loads from VOUTB and/or VSYS.
- **Grounds:** PVSS (pin 23) and the exposed pad AVSS (pin 25) must both connect to the ground plane; keep
  high-current VBUS/VBAT/SW loops tight (PCB guidelines p.54).
- **VBUS abs-max 20 V:** if USB VBUS can see transients/charger faults, OVP handles to 20 V but external clamping
  may be warranted; never exceed 20 V abs-max.
- **CHG/ERR (pins 8/10):** regulated 5 mA low-side LED drivers; connect indicator LEDs directly from VSYS to these pins without series resistors. If shared with a host GPIO, verify the host's
  input voltage tolerance (datasheet note, p.25).
- **No I2C/SPI:** there is no comms bus to the PMIC — fuel-gauging/battery telemetry on this board comes from the
  separate MAX17048, not the nPM1100. Don't expect register access.
